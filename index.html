Game Technology

This was my first minor. In this minor the main focus was more on the programming side of making games and less on designing it. The subjects I had during this minor where:
- Design Patterns
- Advanced Physics
- Graphics Programming
- Research Design GT

I also worked on a project with 6 other students. This project was making a game with C++ with an engine of choice. We chose for the Irrlicht engine.

The game we worked on was race game with Tron like bike's. We decided to give a twist to the racing aspect by making the track less visible the faster you drove. Also, over time the visibilty became worse over time to enfore the player to go for pick-ups to increase their visibilty again. There were more types of power-ups like a speed boost. Further did we decide on having both the player and the AI have a trail that will always glow but of a certain length. This is to make it a bit easier on the player and he/she is able to see how the track curves based on the trail of your opponents.

Graduation Internship

For my graduation, I worked for the Smart Education Lab of the AUAS. The project that I worked on is a project for TransEET. My work consisted of making an prototype for an Educational Game that makes use of Augmented Reality. This prototype is meant to learn the basics of geometric formulas of surfaces of 3D geometrical objects. The objective is to make 3D geometrical figures dissapear by using the correct formula per surface. Wether the player used the incorrect or correct formula, the color of the surfaces will change as feedback as well as each surface will tell the player, what formula they used on that surface. If a surface was correct, it will dissapear after a few seconds to give view of more surfaces. From several iterations, much feedback has been collected which can be used to improve the prototype in the future. A video demo can be found here. This prototype was also presented at two differnt symposiums. The first symposium was the Second International Symposium on Augmented and Virtual Reality in Mathematics Education. The second symposium was the Symposium Smart Education. At both symposium there was interest in the prototype.
