Playtests

From the first playtest it was clear that the your charge bar what indicates how much charge you have left was not clear enough and overlooked in the beginning. Further was it not clear if you picked up a key or not. Lastly the area that gives you charge back looked just like enemy or magnet so the player thought he had to do something with it. But in fact you need to stand against it to get some charge.

Game Technology

This was my first minor. In this minor the main focus was more on the programming side of making games and less on designing it. The subjects I had during this minor where:
- Design Patterns
- Advanced Physics
- Graphics Programming
- Research Design GT

I also worked on a project with 6 other students. This project was making a game with C++ with an engine of choice. We chose for the Irrlicht engine.

The game we worked on was race game with Tron like bike's. We decided to give a twist to the racing aspect by making the track less visible the harder you drove. Aswell as removing a bit of the visibilty over time to enforce the player to go for pick-ups. There were more types of power-ups like a speed boost. Further did we decide on having both the player and the AI have a trail that will always glow but of a certain length. This is to make it a bit easier on the player. And he/she is able to see how the track curves based on the trail of the AI.
